# Facilitator Observation Guide

## Purpose

The observation rubric measures performance during team missions without turning the facilitator into a second answer key. It captures what the team actually does and says.

## Five Observable Behaviors

1. Control of funds: counts before acceptance, safeguards accountability, and stops when evidence is incomplete.
2. Form reasoning: explains what the record proves and connects entries to the accountable event.
3. Calculation accuracy: builds book balance, counts physical funds, uses signed differences, and selects the correct foreign-currency branch.
4. Role handoff: states who transfers what, to whom, under which authority, with which evidence.
5. Balance explanation: states balanced, shortage, overage, or noncash adjustment and identifies the next control action.

## Rating Scale

- 1: Needs coaching.
- 2: Performs.
- 3: Explains and adapts.

Every dimension requires a rating. Facilitators should record a short piece of observed evidence instead of relying on a general impression.

## Readiness Logic

- 13 to 15 points, with strong explanation and acceptable control of funds: ready to lead with oversight.
- 10 to 12 points, with acceptable explanation and control of funds: ready with normal supervision.
- Below 10 points or weak control/explanation: targeted remediation required.

The rubric suggests the coaching response, but the facilitator records the final decision.

## Data Handling

Team names, ratings, and notes remain in local browser storage. Use fictional team names if the device is shared beyond the event. Do not enter PII, operational details, or sensitive financial identifiers.
