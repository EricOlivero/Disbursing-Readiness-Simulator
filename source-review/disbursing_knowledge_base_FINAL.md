# 1. Roles and Responsibilities

## Joint Financial Management

**Type:** Training practice derived from authoritative references.

Joint Financial Management supports the Joint Force Commander through resource management and finance support functions. DoD components involved include OSD, Military Departments, Joint Staff, Combatant Commands, DoD IG, DoD agencies, and DoD field activities. Citation: `57_ 37.22 Joint Deployed Operations.pdf`, pp.3–4, 8, 10–12.

## Disbursing Officers and Accountable Officials

**Type:** Authoritative requirement excerpt.

Disbursing Officers are responsible for safeguarding public funds and are accountable for losses and deficiencies associated with those funds. Citation: `393_ A2232_Safe_combination_change.pdf`, p.2.

**Type:** Authoritative requirement excerpt.

Accountable officials appointed under DD Form 577 are pecuniarily liable for losses, deficiencies, public funds, vouchers, checks, securities, and records entrusted to them. Citation: `210_ A1134_PA_Appt.pdf`, p.2.

# 2. Appointments and Authority

## DD Form 577

**Type:** Authoritative requirement excerpt.

DD Form 577 is used to appoint accountable officials. Appointees acknowledge acceptance of responsibilities in Section III. Citation: `205_ A1110_Cashier_Appt.pdf`, p.2.

## Paying Agent Appointment

**Type:** Scenario exercise with authoritative excerpt.

Paying Agents must present a DD Form 577 appointment and are accountable officials. Citation: `210_ A1134_PA_Appt.pdf`, pp.2,4.

## Unresolved Appointment Authority Conflict

**Type:** Unresolved issue.

Evidence conflicts regarding who appoints Disbursing Agents and cashiers:

- DDO Operations material states DAs are appointed by the DO.
- Training 37.1 narrative references contingency commander appointment.
- Training slide references DO appointment.
- Cashier Appointment scenario states DA initiates appointment paperwork while embedded excerpt states the DO appoints cashiers.

Citations:
- `44_ 36. DDO Ops.pdf`, pp.20,25
- `41_ Appointment of Agents.pdf`, pp.3,9
- `205_ A1110_Cashier_Appt.pdf`, pp.1–2

No resolution exists in the controlling evidence set.

# 3. DDO, Cashier, Disbursing Agent, and Paying Agent Operations

## Cashier Restrictions

**Type:** Authoritative requirement excerpt.

Cashiers may not:
- Sign checks.
- Prepare vouchers they later pay.
- Prepare vouchers on days they perform cashier functions.
- Accept checks drawn or endorsed to themselves in certain accountable capacities.

Citation: `205_ A1110_Cashier_Appt.pdf`, p.2.

## Cashier Advance Operations

**Type:** Scenario practice supported by authoritative excerpt.

Cashiers receive advances using DD Form 1081 and count all funds bill-by-bill before accepting accountability. Citations:
- `206_ A1111_Cashier_Advance.pdf`, pp.1–3
- `395_ A2300_Cashier_Advance.pdf`, pp.1–3

## Paying Agent Operations

**Type:** Scenario practice.

Paying Agents may deploy to support bare-base operations and must possess valid appointment documentation before conducting accountable activities. Citation: `210_ A1134_PA_Appt.pdf`, pp.1–4.

# 4. DD Form 1081 Applications

## Official Use

**Type:** Authoritative requirement excerpt.

DOs advance cash for official use to DDOs, agents, and cashiers using DD Form 1081. Citation: `206_ A1111_Cashier_Advance.pdf`, p.2.

## Required Controls

**Type:** Assessment standard.

Validation requires:
- Foreign currency expressed in U.S. dollar equivalent.
- “On Advance” identified.
- Amounts entered under Increase.
- Matching counts between issuer and recipient.

Citations:
- `206_ A1111_Cashier_Advance.pdf`, p.3
- `395_ A2300_Cashier_Advance.pdf`, p.3

## Change Fund Applications

**Type:** Authoritative requirement excerpt.

DD Form 1081 is used to advance and return change funds. Citation: `379_ A2210_Change_Fund_Appointment.pdf`, p.2.

# 6. Accountability, Advances, and Turn-Ins

## Accountability for Funds

**Type:** Authoritative requirement excerpt.

Un-negotiated checks and funds with agents are reported as cash on hand or equivalent accountability items. Citations:
- `206_ A1111_Cashier_Advance.pdf`, p.2
- `395_ A2300_Cashier_Advance.pdf`, p.2

## Continuity During Incapacity or Arrest

**Type:** Authoritative requirement excerpt.

When a DO/DDO dies, becomes incapacitated, is removed, arrested, or is absent, commanders may seize and secure disbursing assets, appoint an inventory board, and establish custodial accountability until inventory is completed.

Citation: `382_ A2219_DA_Arrested.pdf`, pp.2–4.

# 7. Safeguarding Funds, Vouchers, and Negotiable Instruments

## Physical Security

**Type:** Authoritative requirement excerpt.

Public funds must be stored in secured containers and protected from unauthorized access. Excess cash should be deposited promptly. Restricted-access controls should be maintained.

Citation: `392_ A2231_Open-Close_Safe_1.pdf`, p.2.

## Safe Combinations

**Type:** Authoritative requirement excerpt.

Safe combinations must be changed at least every six months and upon relief, transfer, separation, or discharge of accountable personnel.

Citations:
- `392_ A2231_Open-Close_Safe_1.pdf`, p.3
- `393_ A2232_Safe_combination_change.pdf`, p.3

# 8. Foreign Currency and Exchange / Reverse Exchange

## Revaluation Loss

**Type:** Scenario exercise with authoritative excerpt.

Where Treasury establishes a prevailing rate, gains and deficiencies are computed and recorded using prescribed accountability procedures. The scenario demonstrates a $75 revaluation loss.

Citation: `276_ A2312a_Confused_Agent.pdf`, pp.2–3.

## Average Purchase Rate

**Type:** Scenario exercise with authoritative excerpt.

Where no Treasury prevailing rate exists and foreign currency is purchased locally, an Average Purchase Rate is computed and documented using a Certificate of Change.

Citation: `277_ A2312b_Confused_Agent.pdf`, pp.1–4.

## Unresolved Branch Separation

**Type:** Unresolved issue.

The evidence specifically directs that revaluation-loss processing and Average Purchase Rate processing remain separate and not be merged.

Citations:
- `276_ A2312a_Confused_Agent.pdf`, pp.1–3
- `277_ A2312b_Confused_Agent.pdf`, pp.1–4

# 11. Counterfeit and Altered Instruments

## Counterfeit U.S. Currency

**Type:** Authoritative requirement excerpt.

When counterfeit or altered U.S. currency is identified before acceptance:
- Confiscate the item.
- Provide a receipt.
- Obtain source information.
- Deliver it to appropriate investigative or security authorities.
- Obtain a receipt from receiving authorities.

Citation: `216_ A1114_Counterfeit_Bill.pdf`, p.2.

## Accounting Treatment

**Type:** Authoritative requirement excerpt.

No accounting entry is required when counterfeit currency is detected before acceptance.

Citation: `216_ A1114_Counterfeit_Bill.pdf`, p.2.

## Unresolved Foreign Currency Handling

**Type:** Unresolved issue.

The controlling evidence does not reconcile counterfeit U.S. currency procedures with counterfeit foreign-currency procedures.

Citations:
